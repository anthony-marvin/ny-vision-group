About Premium Cataract Surgery

A cataract is a clouding of the eye's natural lens whereby images seem less crisp and vivid.  The condition can be compared to a window that is fogged over with steam or darkened by soot. Cataracts are usually associated with the normal aging process and are the leading causes of vision loss for adults over 55.

The general treatment for cataracts is cataract surgery, one of the safest and most effective procedures available today. Traditionally, an artificial monofocal lens would be used to replace the natural clouded lens. These monofocal lenses can also correct somewhat for distance.

In the last decade, however, cataract surgery has undergone numerous advances which have not only made the procedure faster but also shortened the length of recovery time. Now a cataract procedure typically takes 10 minutes and is performed on an outpatient basis.

Equally important, new premium lens implants such as the Tecnis Symfony, ReStor and Crystalens have been developed which allow for a broader range of distance.  In addition to these multifocal lenses, there are lenses such as the Acrysof Toric which can correct astigmatism.  Finally, new premium surgical tools such as ORA, an innovative measurement system used during the surgery, and FEMTO-laser assisted cataract surgery, has been developed to help further refine results.  In the hands of skilled surgeons such as Dr. Koster, these advances mean superior results for patients.

Premium Lens Options

Premium Lens Implants offer a new alternative for cataract patients seeking a broader range of vision than the vision provided by traditional “mono focal” lens implants. Recent technological breakthroughs have led to the development of several exciting new options for cataract patients, including:

  • Multifocal lenses such as the Tecnis Multifocal and ReStor lens implants, designed with built-in multi-focality allowing the eye to focus at both distance and near;
  • Extended Depth of Focus lenses such as the Tecnis Symfony lens implants, designed to provide continuous, high-quality intermediate and distance;
  • Accommodating lenses such as the Crystalens lens implants, designed to move with your eye as you focus from distance to near.

In addition, NY Vision Group offers the Astigmatic-Correcting Toric lens implants to help reduce or eliminate astigmatism after cataract surgery. We specialize in using Optiware Refractive Analysis (a unique laser measurement system of the eye during cataract surgery, used to check and fine-tune intraocular measurements) and FEMTO laser-assisted cataract surgery. This revolutionary technology allows Dr. Koster to use a laser to make all necessary incisions, correct astigmatism and soften the cataract prior to removal.
